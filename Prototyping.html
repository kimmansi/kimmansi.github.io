Prototyping

All the prototyping steps

From sketching wireframes, to converting them to low-fi and then high-fi wireframes in Figma, to then linking all the nodes to create the prototype.

Wireframe sketch. Sketch of the coffee house main landing page for mobile and one of the product pages.





The low fidelity wireframe. I converted the page layouts I worked out in the sketches into low-fi mockups. This stage helped decide the final form for the main page. And also helped determine some of the tweaks I had to make on the About Us page.




The high fidelity wireframe in Figma. I was able to finalize the color scheme and thankfully didn't need to change anything too much from the original plan for that. I did have to tweak the original logo to work with the header as planned. The hi-fi wireframe helped to clearly point out where that would have caused issues once I started coding this site. This was also the stage when I started deciding which features I would put in on each frame to make at least one element fun.

Linking the prototype. Linking frames and elements within the frames was the final step of creating the prototype in Figma. I had to do some tweaks here and there to make sure the flow worked. This was also the stage where I went back and added the animated hamburger menu and slide in navigation.


The prototype. You can view the Figma prototype for the coffee house project here. The final frame almost exactly matched the original wireframe sketch and the planning of that helped a lot with creating the Order, Shop, and Meet sections of the page. I did have to adjust the other frames more from the original sketches though. But overall the low fidelity wireframe designs held up all the way to the final prototype.
