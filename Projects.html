The Coffee House Project. My first project that was taken from moodboard stage, to prototype, to responsive website. The business mission statement was a sophisticated coffee house that specialized in artisan handcrafted coffee drinks but that was also very focused on community involvement and saw themselves as a place for locals to call their own. That influenced my color choices a lot. In addition to choosing elements for the logo that gave a softer, more welcoming appeal. The DIVINE Investments Project. The client project. The black, white, and pink color scheme was already part of the exisiting brand for the client. I updated and balanced with a deep plum and medium gray for the website color palette. The logo was a balance of hard and soft, the circle surrounding the hard edges of the buildings and the triangle, while keeping the pink and pink triangle the client identified with their brand for the divine feminine. I chose the graduated height structure for the buildings and the starting dark window flowing to the top floor lit one as visual representations of the growth this company provides it's clients. I continued that balance of hard and soft throughout the website to ensure that the business would attract it's target client: investors who aren't investors yet and who are underserved by other investment companies because they don't make their business approachable. Investing for everyone was the overriding theme of this website and branding. You can view the website here or you can click on the picture to learn more about this project.

The Cat Rescue Project. An unexpected opportunity to take the first steps exploring my passion side product: creating a template for animal rescue sites to use to help them better manage adoptions and also volunteer and donation flow and coordination. This site was created to try to help bring focus on the feral cat rescue efforts that are ongoing in the Lehigh Valley and also to help the loosely organized efforts of the rescuers find a central point to collect information and ask for the public's help.
